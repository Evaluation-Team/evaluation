Build your team

Evaluation requires a range of different skills and knowledge, so strongly benefits from having a multidisciplinary team. This guide is written so that people who don’t necessarily have a background in evaluation, can still evaluate a digital health intervention. If you’re reading this, that could be you.

The people on your team will likely have varying degrees of evaluation expertise and experience. Someone who has no evaluation experience can still conduct and take part in a lot of evaluation activities, for example: creating a logic model. It’s a good idea to reach out to colleagues that have more experience, to support in an advisory role.

You can use the capabilities below to help decide who may be best suited to leading or contributing to your evaluation, as well as understanding where there might be skills gaps in your team.

Evaluator capabilities

A proficient evaluator can design and oversee an evaluation by:

  • providing a clear rationale for conducting an evaluation and identifies the risks of not carrying out an evaluation
  • setting out an appropriate and sufficient budget for evaluation and clearly outlines a rationale for the evaluation budget including people, resources and time needed
  • designing and planning evaluation into a project from the start
  • ensuring that the team completes a service for the journey for the DHI to inform the completion of a logic model
  • leading on the completion of a logic model by the evaluation team and identifies relevant short, medium, and long term outcomes
  • overseeing the prioritisation of outcomes in order to select appropriate success indicators and provides a rationale for indicators selected
  • selecting evaluation methods that will be used to measure indicators of success and provides a rationale for methods chosen – acknowledging the importance of using a mixed-methods evaluation approach
