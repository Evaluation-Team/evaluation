Build your team

Evaluation requires a range of different skills and knowledge, so benefits from having a multidisciplinary team.

Your team will likely have varying degrees of evaluation expertise and experience. Someone who has no evaluation experience can still conduct and take part in many evaluation activities, such as creating a logic model.

Experience and capabilities

You should reach out to colleagues with evaluation experience, as they can provide support for your project and help fill skills gaps in your team.

It’s a good idea to decide who can lead the evaluation activities in your team. To lead the evaluation activities in your team, you don’t have to have prior evaluation experience. Use this guidance to design and oversee the evaluation of your health product.

Planning the evaluation of your health product

You should always design evaluation into the project from the start.

Decide how extensive your evaluation should be. Ensure your team has an appropriate amount of budget, people and time available to carry out the evaluation of your health product as intended.

Managing the evaluation of your health product

Ensure that your team completes a user journey for your health product, so you can then complete a logic model and identify your health product’s intended outcomes.

You should oversee the prioritisation of outcomes, so your team can select appropriate success indicators and say why you selected your chosen indicators.

They must also lead your team in deciding which evaluation methods you’ll use to measure your success indicators. Your team should provide a rationale for the methods chosen and acknowledge the importance of using a mixed-methods evaluation approach.
