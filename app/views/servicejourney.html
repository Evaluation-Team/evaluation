Map your service journey

What is a service journey?

A service journey is a simple timeline from left to right, showing how your users interact with the digital health intervention at different stages. You can then link these interactions to the health outcomes your digital health intervention is trying to create.

Why create a service journey?

Use a service journey to get an overview of how your digital health intervention meets user needs and achieves its intended health outcomes over time. Creating a service journey will help you to align your user needs, interactions with the service actions and health outcomes.

Case study: Couch to 5K

The Couch to 5K team started by considering their users' needs at each stage of interacting with the app – from being completely unaware of it to stopping using it, and important touchpoints between.

This helped them think about how the app's activities meet those needs – and how they could affect health outcomes.

For example, the team knew their users need to stay motivated when using the app. To meet this need, the app provides motivational messages and bonus incentives. This targets the health outcome 'user is confident enough to go for a run'.

How to map your service journey

Step 1: Map the user’s journey through your service

You can create a service journey for an existing digital health intervention, or for an entirely new one. You can create a service journey during a face-to-face or online workshop.

The evaluation lead for your digital health intervention or another team member should facilitate the service journey workshop and ensure the outputs are written up and shared with the team.

Your research should help you understand who your users are, their needs and how they could potentially use your digital health intervention.

You should work with your stakeholders to understand the different stages of your service journey, and which user needs are met at each stage. As you do this, map the relevant user needs to the appropriate stage.

This activity will help you understand the digital health intervention from the user’s point of view, and establish the most important touchpoints in their journey.

Step 2: Map the health outcomes to the service journey

Once you know the health outcomes your digital health intervention could impact, you can start thinking about how these relate to the user and the service journey.

You then need to map the short, medium and long-term health outcomes from your logic model to the service journey.

Don’t worry if you can’t map all of the health outcomes. Some medium and long-term health outcomes, such as ‘reduction in cardiovascular diseases in the United Kingdom’, will not be measurable in the journey through your service.

Step 3: Map the service actions to the service journey

As you develop your digital health intervention, you will need to reach a clear understanding of your service’s actions, or what your service does for the user, at each stage of the service journey.

Working with your team, bring together all the information you have about what the service does and map these service actions to the service journey.
