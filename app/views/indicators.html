Choose success indicators

What is an indicator?

It is a marker that shows the effect of the intervention. Health indicators are the measurable signs of the effect of your digital health intervention is having on people’s health and wellbeing.

For example, Couch to 5K, a physical activity app wanted to measure how people engaged with their intervention. One of their chosen indicators was ‘uptake of the service’. They also wanted to understand the impact they were having on health, so another indicator was ‘number of people with a healthier BMI after using the app’.

Why choose success indicators?

Establishing the most appropriate indicators will help you to understand what impact your digital health intervention is having. Understanding the impact will help you iterate the design of the intervention and better improve your chosen health outcomes.

Case study: Couch to 5K

When the Couch to 5K team were looking for relevant indicators, they first used the library of indicators.

Here, they chose indicators relevant to their project – like the proportion of adults doing 30 to 145 minutes of physical activity per week. This indicator could help them prove that they’d promoted physical activity in the wider population.

Using indicators that are common across other health services meant that the intervention could be compared to others.

How to choose your indicators

Prioritising health outcomes

The indicators you choose will be closely related to your chosen health outcomes. Each health outcome is likely to have multiple indicators to understand whether it's being met or not. If you don’t already know which health outcomes your digital health intervention targets, then creating a logic model with your team is the best way to identify them.

When deciding which outcomes to focus on, consider:

  • what you can practically measure
  • which outcomes could have the most direct impact on your project’s aims
  • the priorities of your organisation, stakeholders or partners

    • For each outcome you want to measure, you’ll need at least one indicator.

Using existing indicators

Rather than creating your own indicators, it’s useful to choose existing indicators.

This will help you compare your digital health intervention to other health interventions, and it’s more likely that data will already be available.
